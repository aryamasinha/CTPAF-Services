Testimonials


Ankita Ghoshal


I have spent most of my professional career in programming before switching to Data Science. I studied statistics as a kid but those concepts were long forgotten. Making this transition was indeed tough, but not impossible. The key is to never stop learning. During this switch, I realized that you don’t need to unlearn your existing skills to pick up a new one. I used my programming skills as a bridge between IT and Data Science to structure my machine learning code more logically. This transition also helped me understand that the presentation of project results varies significantly from industry to industry. For example, in the IT industry, the output of a web development project is a web page that is completely understandable by the stakeholders. In the world of data science, the output is (usually) numbers. It is the role of a data science professional to reveal these numbers to the customers/stakeholders using an indicative story.



Rachel Zhiqing Zheng


After six months’ planning, training and preparation, I started my new career journey as a data analyst recently during the COVID-19 period. I would like to share my journey and experience here for those that would like to take the path less chosen. As a procurement professional in a FMCG company for the past five years, I decided to change my career in late 2019. My goal was to land in a job within the IT industry, related to data science. I did not expect such changes to happen over night and six to nine months is a good estimate. I was also prepared to handle the financial stress, which meant six to nine months of zero income.
